Sometimes, people ask me what I'd like to receive for a holiday. Here are some ideas, which I hope will be helpful (and so I don't say "I don't know!").



I like consumable things that create experiences rather than more stuff.

  • Unique/specialty condiments, like jams, honeys, salts, peppers, sauces, etc. I don't like pickles, though.
  • Candles/essential oils, I often burn candles in the cooler months, and use an aroma diffuser regularly, and I like fresh, herbal, and woodsy notes. Please nothing soapy, powdery, or floral.
  • Fabric always gets used eventually, even though my exact interest in sewing waxes and wanes and wends through various hobbies (costuming, quilting, apparel, etc). I strongly prefer 100% natural fibers (linen, silk, cotton, wool), and classic/classy patterns or designs.
    • 1 yard is useful as a quilting fabric or a tank top
    • 2-3 yards is a typical minimum for apparel projects like blouses, button-ups, tank tops, pants, etc., and a good quantity for a quilting project
    • 4-6 yards is a dress or coat
    • 6-20 yards is sufficient for a costume/fantasy/historic gown, which rates low on a practicality scale but high on the fun- to-make-and-wear-for-photos scale. This much fabric in a natural fiber is a generous gift; feel free to check in with me to make sure it's a color or material I will be delighted by.
